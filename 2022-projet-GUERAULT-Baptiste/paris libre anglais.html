Free Paris 1871 by Jacques Rougerie :

The main actor of this book is the city of Paris, insurgent in 1871, "free" Paris as it was called then. The author treats the city as a being in its own right, moving, rebelling, liberating itself, collapsing... He gives the floor to the witnesses of the time, introduces them with a quick commentary and then proposes some lines of thought to help the reader measure the meaning and scope of the events.

The main phases of the "terrible year" are presented, such as the maturation of the idea of revolt in war-torn Paris, the uprising in the capital and the liberation of the city before its crushing. In passing, the author paints a realistic and colorful portrait of the "communeux".
